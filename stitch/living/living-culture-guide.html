Guide for Expats

The 'Thai Way'

Pattaya is a vibrant international city, but the foundation of social harmony remains rooted in traditional Thai values. Mastering these cultural nuances ensures a respectful and rewarding residency.

check_circle Cultural Dos

  • The Wai Protocol

    Bow with palms together to show respect to elders, officials, and new acquaintances.

  • Social Hierarchy

    Acknowledge age and status with appropriate gestures and a calm, polite tone of voice.

  • Dress Codes

    Wear modest clothing (covering shoulders and knees) when visiting temples and official buildings.

  • Respect for Monks

    Give up your seat and maintain a respectful distance; women should avoid any physical contact.

cancel Cultural Don'ts

  • Touching the Head

    Considered the most sacred part of the body; avoid touching anyone's head, even children.

  • Pointing Feet

    Feet are seen as the lowest part of the body; never point them at people, monks, or images of the Buddha.

  • Raising Voice

    Maintain a calm demeanor; losing your temper is seen as 'losing face' and is highly counterproductive.

  • Disrespecting the Monarchy

    Always show utmost respect for the Royal Family. Discussion of the monarchy is subject to strict laws.
